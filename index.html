Gaming Universe

Adventure

Graphic Adventures: Games with a strong emphasis on storytelling and problem-solving, often featuring point-and-click interfaces. Examples: The Secret of Monkey Island, The Last of Us.

Action-Adventure: Games that combine elements of action and adventure, often involving exploration, combat, and puzzle-solving. Examples: Uncharted, Assassin's Creed.

Action

Platformers: Games that involve jumping, running, and climbing through levels, often avoiding obstacles or enemies. Examples: Super Mario Bros., Celeste.

Shooters: Games where players use firearms or other ranged weapons to combat enemies. Examples: Call of Duty, Halo.

Fighting Games: Games focused on hand-to-hand combat, often featuring competitive multiplayer modes. Examples: Street Fighter, Mortal Kombat.

Simulation

Sports Games: Games that simulate various sports, allowing players to compete against AI or other players. Examples: FIFA, NBA 2K

Racing Games: Games that focus on vehicle racing, featuring realistic physics and tracks. Examples: Gran Turismo, Forza Horizon.

Life Simulation Games: Simulate aspects of real life. Examples: The Sims, Cities: Skylines.

Role-Playing

Open-World RPGs: Games that involve jumping, running, and climbing through levels, often avoiding obstacles or enemies. Examples: Super Mario Bros., Celeste.

Turn-Based RPGs: Games where players use firearms or other ranged weapons to combat enemies. Examples: Call of Duty, Halo.

Character Creation: Players often have a high degree of customization, creating unique characters with specific attributes, skills, and backgrounds.
